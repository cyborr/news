Los Mambises, a civic movement to boycott Cuban government businesses

Los Mambises is the name of a peaceful civic struggle movement, created this December with the purpose of boycotting the Cuban government’s businesses from social networks. Its founder is Cuban activist based in Canada, Alberto Fonseca, who earlier this month protested, together with a friend, over the sale of Cubita coffee in stores in the city of Vancouver, a product associated with the state company CIMEX and the GAESA military conglomerate.

Fire at ETECSA headquarters leaves eastern Cuba incommunicado

Groups of firefighters were mobilized to attend the fire that has caused a technological blackout. (Foto: Gretel Yanet-Facebook) A fire broke out at the ETECSA headquarters in Las Tunas province on Thursday afternoon, leaving that Cuban territory completely incommunicado and partially affecting others. According to the report by journalist Gretel Yanet, from the Provincial Radio Victoria Station, the incident at the territorial headquarters of the telecommunications monopoly in Cuba left that part of the eastern part of the country in a technological blackout, by paralyzing the internet service and mobile data, as well as radio and television signals.

Fire destroys nine houses in Santiago de Cuba
